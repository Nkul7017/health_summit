Overview

Redefining Indian Healthcare Ecosystem

The recently-held second Global Investors Summit in Dehradun, Uttarakhand, witnessed investment proposals worth more than Rs 3.5 lakh being received by the hill state. Memoranda of Understanding (MoUs) to the tune of Rs 44,0000 crore have already got inked. With healthcare and pharma emerging as one of the focus sectors, the vertical is clearly at an inflection point. However, before the sector moves to the next level of growth, it has to overcome several existing bottlenecks endemic to the state.

There is an acute shortage of doctors and health personnel, especially in the hilly districts. Their retention is an even bigger challenge. The high maternal mortality and child mortality rates have to be reduced. The soaring incidence of noncommunicable and communicable diseases has to be brought under control and above all, ensure universal health coverage.

Read More...

In the pharmaceutical sector, among other priority areas, there is a need to focus on innovation so as to move up the value chain, create a strong innovation pipeline with new product launches and molecular entities every year, and develop a comprehensive and efficient supply chain management model.

With the deadline for Sustainable Development Goals (SDG 3) approaching in 2030, there is a dire need to relook at the current approaches and come up with futuristic, innovative and disruptive strategies for the sector.

Why the Summit?

  • It is crucial to build upon the strong foundation set for the sector by the Investors Summit.
  • The 1st Healthcare & Pharma Summit will play the role of a catalyst in this direction.
  • It will bring all the stakeholders on a common platform, thereby enabling comprehensive discussions on industry challenges, strategies for plugging existing gaps, and eventually providing a clear vision to build a robust, accessible and efficient healthcare and pharma sector for all in Uttarakhand.
